##  What This Project Is About ??

NovaMart is a full UI/UX design project for a multi-vendor
e-commerce platform. The platform serves three groups of people
— customers who want to find and buy things quickly, vendors who
need simple tools to manage their stores, and admins who keep
everything running.

The main problem I was trying to solve: most Indian e-commerce
apps lose more than 70% of shoppers before they finish buying.
The checkout is too long, vendor onboarding is confusing, and
the overall experience feels overwhelming. NovaMart is designed
to fix all three of those things.

##  Usability Testing Results

Two rounds of testing were done — Round 1 on wireframes
with 24 participants, Round 2 on the full prototype with
18 participants.

| Flow | Round 1 | Round 2 | Improvement |
|------|---------|---------|-------------|
| Complete checkout | 54% | 87% | +33 points |
| Vendor onboarding | 43% | 81% | +38 points |
| Order tracking | 71% | 91% | +20 points |
| Product search | 78% | 90% | +12 points |
| Homepage navigation | 92% | 96% | +4 points |

The biggest improvement was in vendor onboarding — from 43%
to 81%. The change that made the most difference was replacing
a plain form with a guided 4-step wizard that had photo upload
tips and inline error messages.

##  Accessibility

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliant throughout
- Minimum colour contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for all body text
- 3:1 minimum for large text and UI components
- Full keyboard navigation on checkout flow
- ARIA labels on all icon-only buttons
- Visible focus states on every interactive element
- Colour is never the only way meaning is communicated

---

##  Dark Mode

Full dark mode implemented across all screens with a
separate set of colour tokens — not just an inversion
of the light mode. Both modes tested against WCAG
contrast ratios.

##  Key Learnings

- Testing on wireframes early saved a lot of rework later
- Building the design system before touching screens was
  the right call — slowed down day 1 but sped up every
  day after
- Vendor complexity was underestimated at the start —
  more vendor interviews in week 1 would have helped
- Dark mode needs to be planned from the beginning,
  not added at the end
- Real user quotes from testing are more useful than
  any persona card
